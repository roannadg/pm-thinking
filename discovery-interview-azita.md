# Interview Summary — Azita Deljo
**Date**: Late March 2026
**Participants**: Azita Deljo (Clinical Psychologist, solo private practice), Roanna Gunaratnam, Haifa
**Background**: 20+ years experience. Bilingual English/Farsi. Referral-only practice. Transitioning into neurofeedback/neurotherapy (rebranded as Yarra Valley Clinical Psychology and Neurotherapy). Uses Halaxy for practice management. Lost receptionist post-COVID, managing all admin herself since.

---

## Current Solution
Halaxy for practice management (invoicing, billing, online payments, AI note-taking available but unused). Personal Word templates for clinical reports. Manual EEG data review with paid mentor ($200/hour) for neurofeedback sessions.

---

## What Works
- Halaxy online payments handle ~90% of billing automatically
- Personal report templates are efficient (took years to develop)
- Handwritten notes during sessions help organise clinical thinking — a deliberate practice tool, not just admin
- Halaxy has a responsive feedback loop — requests are acted on

---

## Problems

### Billing follow-up — HIGH pain, LOW satisfaction
The 10% of invoices that fail automatic processing require personal follow-up with Medicare, TAC, or clients. Azita puts it off — sometimes for 2 years. Real money delayed or lost. Each case is different (different funding sources: Medicare, TAC, NDIS, private) so it requires individual attention.

> "I'm a psychologist — I love doing what I do. I don't like spending 30 minutes chasing an invoice for an hour session."

Would value an AI equivalent of a bookkeeper — something that identifies what's unpaid, why, and how to resolve it.

### Report writing — MEDIUM pain, MEDIUM satisfaction
Clinical reports are time-consuming. Templates help but writing is still slow. Notes in Farsi limit AI tools. Young practitioners likely use AI here already. Azita doesn't because she has templates and doesn't trust unsupervised AI output.

### EEG data interpretation for neurofeedback — HIGH pain, NO current solution
Session-to-session, Azita needs to track whether brain wave patterns are moving in the right direction. Currently manual — naked eye review of data or $200/hour mentor. AI tools exist but are expensive and inaccessible. 

> "I know AI does a much better job than someone who's had 30 years of practice in this area, and much quicker."

She will buy neurofeedback software (~$10,000) in 1-2 months and offered to collaborate on building something for this.

### AI report quality in the market — CONCERN, not personal pain
Clients submitting AI-generated psychology reports for her to sign. Look professional but contain factual errors an experienced clinician would catch. Risk: junior practitioners may not catch errors.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Biggest immediate pain is billing admin, not report writing.** Templates solve report writing. Billing follow-up has no good solution.
2. **Neurofeedback is the future-facing opportunity.** No good AI tool exists for interpreting EEG progress session-to-session. Azita would open her practice to collaboration.
3. **Language is a real barrier.** Farsi notes mean most AI tools don't work. Multilingual solution unlocks this segment.
4. **Trust gap on AI quality is real.** AI as co-pilot, not replacement. Any tool positioning as autonomous will lose clinicians like Azita. Right framing: AI surfaces the pattern, clinician makes the call.
5. **Halaxy has built a moat.** Any admin AI tool needs to integrate with Halaxy or fill a gap Halaxy hasn't. The gaps: failed billing follow-up and EEG interpretation layer.
